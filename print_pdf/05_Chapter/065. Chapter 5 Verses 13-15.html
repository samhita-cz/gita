Chapter 5 — karma sanyāsa yoga

065. Chapter 5 Verses 13-15

Lectures by Swami Sarvapriyananda
Verse 5.13
सर्वकर्माणि मनसा संन्यस्यास्ते सुखं वशी ।
नवद्वारे पुरे देही नैव कुर्वन्न कारयन् ।। 13।।
sarvakarmāṇi manasā
sannyasyāste sukhaṃ vaśī |
navadvāre pure dehī
naiva kurvanna kārayan ||
◈ sarva-karmāṇi – all actions; manasā – by the mind;
◈ sannyasya – having renounced; āste – rests; sukham – happily; vaśī – the self-controlled;
◈ nava-dvāre – nine-gated; pure – in the city; dehī – the embodied soul;
◈ na eva – neither; kurvan – acting; na – nor; kārayan – causing to act.
Mentally renouncing all actions and self-controlled, the embodied being, rests happily in the nine-gated city (body) neither acting nor causing others to act.
Verse 5.14
न कर्तृत्वं न कर्माणि लोकस्य सृजति प्रभु: ।
न कर्मफलसंयोगं स्वभावस्तु प्रवर्तते ।। 14।।
na kartṛtvaṃ na karmāṇi
lokasya sṛjati prabhuḥ |
na karmaphalasaṃyogaṃ
svabhāvastu pravartate ||
◈ na – neither; kartṛtvam – agency/doership; na – nor; karmāṇi – actions;
◈ lokasya – of the world; sṛjati – creates; prabhuḥ – the Lord (Self);
◈ na – nor; karma-phala-saṃyogam – union with the fruit of action;
◈ svabhāvaḥ – nature; tu – but; pravartate – operates.
The Lord (Atma) does not create agency, nor action, nor the union of action and its fruit; but Nature leads to action.
Verse 5.15
नादत्ते कस्यचित्पापं न चैव सुकृतं विभु: ।
अज्ञानेनावृतं ज्ञानं तेन मुह्यन्ति जन्तव: ।। 15।।
nādatte kasyacitpāpaṃ
na caiva sukṛtaṃ vibhuḥ |
ajñānenāvṛtaṃ jñānaṃ
tena muhyanti jantavaḥ ||
◈ na ādatte – does not take; kasyacit – anyone's; pāpam – sin;
◈ na – nor; ca eva – and certainly; sukṛtam – merit/virtue; vibhuḥ – the All-pervading Lord;
◈ ajñānena – by ignorance; āvṛtam – is covered; jñānam – knowledge;
◈ tena – by that; muhyanti – are deluded; jantavaḥ – beings.
The Lord does not receive either the evil or good of anyone. Knowledge is enveloped by ignorance, and by it, beings are deluded.

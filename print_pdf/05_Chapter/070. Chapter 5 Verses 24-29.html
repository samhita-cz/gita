Chapter 5 — karma sanyāsa yoga

070. Chapter 5 Verses 24-29

Lectures by Swami Sarvapriyananda
Verse 5.24
योऽन्त:सुखोऽन्तरारामस्तथान्तज्र्योतिरेव य: ।
स योगी ब्रह्मनिर्वाणं ब्रह्मभूतोऽधिगच्छति ।। 24।।
yo’ntaḥsukho’ntarārāmas
tathāntarjyotireva yaḥ |
sa yogī brahmanirvāṇaṃ
brahmabhūto’dhigacchati ||
◈ yaḥ – who; antaḥ-sukhaḥ – happy within; antara-ārāmaḥ – rejoicing within; ◈ tathā – also; antaḥ-jyotiḥ – illuminated within; eva – only; yaḥ – who; ◈ saḥ – that; yogī – Yogi; brahma-nirvāṇam – liberation in Brahman; ◈ brahma-bhūtaḥ – becoming Brahman; adhigacchati – attains.
He who finds happiness within, delights within, and illumined within, that sage becoming Brahman attains absolute perfection.
Verse 5.25
लभन्ते ब्रह्मनिर्वाणमृषय: क्षीणकल्मषा: ।
छिन्नद्वैधा यतात्मान: सर्वभूतहिते रता: ।। 25।।
labhante brahmanirvāṇam
ṛṣayaḥ kṣīṇakalmaṣāḥ |
chinnadvaidhā yatātmānaḥ
sarvabhūtahite ratāḥ ||
◈ labhante – attain; brahma-nirvāṇam – liberation in Brahman; ◈ ṛṣayaḥ – the seers/sages; kṣīṇa-kalmaṣāḥ – whose sins are washed away; ◈ chinnadvaidhāḥ – whose doubts are torn asunder; yata-ātmānaḥ – who are self-controlled; ◈ sarva-bhūta-hite – in the welfare of all beings; ratāḥ – engaged/rejoicing.
Sages whose sins are destroyed, whose dualities are torn asunder, who are self-controlled, who rejoice in the well-being of others, attain union with Brahman (Moksha).
Verse 5.26
कामक्रोधवियुक्तानां यतीनां यतचेतसाम् ।
अभितो ब्रह्मनिर्वाणं वर्तते विदितात्मनाम् ।। 26।।
kāmakrodhaviyuktānāṃ
yatīnāṃ yatacetasām |
abhito brahmanirvāṇaṃ
vartate viditātmanām ||
◈ kāma-krodha-viyuktānām – free from desire and anger; ◈ yatīnām – of the ascetics/seekers; yata-cetasām – of controlled mind; ◈ abhitaḥ – all around/both here and hereafter; brahma-nirvāṇam – liberation in Brahman; ◈ vartate – exists; vidita-ātmanām – for those who have realized the Self.
To the self-controlled sages who are free from desire and wrath, who have controlled their thoughts, who have realised the Self, absolute freedom exists on all sides.
Verse 5.27-28
स्पर्शान्कृत्वा बहिर्बाह्यांश्चक्षुश्चैवान्तरे भ्रुवो: ।
प्राणापानौ समौ कृत्वा नासाभ्यन्तरचारिणौ ।। 27।।
यतेन्द्रियमनोबुद्धिर्मुनिर्मोक्षपरायण: |
विगतेच्छाभयक्रोधो य: सदा मुक्त एव स: || 28||
sparśānkṛtvā bahirbāhyāṃś cakṣuścaivāntare bhruvoḥ | prāṇāpānau samau kṛtvā nāsābhyantaracāriṇau || 27 ||
The sage who has turned away all external impressions, fixing his gaze in the centre of the brows, controlling the incoming and outgoing breath rhythmically, keeping the senses, the mind and the intellect controlled, free from desire, fear and hatred, aspiring for the highest freedom indeed enjoys freedom always.
Verse 5.29
भोक्तारं यज्ञतपसां सर्वलोकमहेश्वरम् ।
सुहृदं सर्वभूतानां ज्ञात्वा मां शान्तिमृच्छति ।। 29।।
bhoktāraṃ yajñatapasāṃ
sarvalokamaheśvaram |
suhṛdaṃ sarvabhūtānāṃ
jñātvā māṃ śāntimṛcchati ||
◈ bhoktāram – the enjoyer; yajña-tapasām – of sacrifices and austerities; ◈ sarva-loka-mahā-īśvaram – the Supreme Lord of all worlds; ◈ suhṛdam – the friend; sarva-bhūtānām – of all beings; ◈ jñātvā – knowing; mām – Me; śāntim – peace; ṛcchati – attains.
Knowing Me as the enjoyer of all sacrifices and austerities, the Lord and controller of all the worlds, friend of all beings, man attains peace.

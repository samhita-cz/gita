Chapter 6 — dhyāna yoga

081. Chapter 6 verses 20-21

Lectures by Swami Sarvapriyananda
Verse 6.20-21
यत्रोपरमते चित्तं निरुद्धं योगसेवया ।
यत्र चैवात्मनात्मानं पश्यन्नात्मनि तुष्यति ।।20।।
yatroparamate cittaṃ
niruddhaṃ yogasevayā |
yatra caivātmanātmānaṃ
paśyannātmani tuṣyati ||
◈ yatra – where; uparamate – becomes quiet/quiescent; cittam – mind; ◈ niruddham – restrained; yoga-sevayā – by the practice of Yoga; ◈ yatra – where; ca eva – and also; ātmanā – by the self (purified mind); ātmānam – the Self; ◈ paśyan – beholding; ātmani – in the Self; tuṣyati – is satisfied.
When the mind (citta), restrained by the practice of yoga (yoga), comes to rest, and when, seeing the self (ātman) by means of the self (ātman), one finds satisfaction in the self (ātman). When one experiences that ultimate happiness which is comprehensible by the intellect (buddhi) and transcends the senses, being established in that truth (tattva), one does not deviate from it.
